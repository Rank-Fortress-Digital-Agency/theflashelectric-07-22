LEDs are semiconductor devices that emit light when an electric current passes through them. They are made up of two materials: a p-type and an n-type. When these two materials are joined together, they create a diode. When electricity flows through the diode, it causes electrons to move from the n-type material to the p-type material. This flow of electrons creates photons (light particles) that exit the diode as light. Because LEDs emit a specific wavelength of light, they are more efficient than other types of bulbs, which produce a range of wavelengths.
